# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary: Telco / ISP executives.** Decision-makers at telecoms and internet service providers evaluating whether to run branded cloud, mail, security, storage, and billing on their own infrastructure — under their own brand — instead of patching together separate vendors or reselling a hyperscaler.

**Secondary: Technical evaluators** (architects, infra leads) who vet the platform's capabilities before an exec signs. Copy and structure must satisfy the exec's "why this, why now, what do I own" while giving the technical reader enough substance (layer breakdown, vendor comparison, go-live steps) to trust the platform.

**Context of use:** a sales / overview pitch consumed during an evaluation — read on a laptop in a meeting or forwarded to a buying committee. It is a deck-as-page, not a product UI.

## Product Purpose

WorldPosta is a complete operator-grade ecosystem that lets service providers run cloud, collaboration, security, billing, and management services on their own infrastructure, under their own brand. The purpose of this page is to convince an operator that WorldPosta is the software layer that lets them own their platform business end-to-end.

## Brand Personality

**Sovereign · Precise · Weighty.** Authoritative and serious — infrastructure-grade backbone the operator *controls*, not a service they rent. The core emotional promise is ownership: this is yours, on your hardware, under your brand. No fluff, no hype, no startup warmth.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages (templated startup look: pricing tiers, smiley stock photos, rounded-everything)
- Crypto / web3 sites (neon-on-black, glowing gradients, hype language)
- Cheap reseller / budget-hosting sites (badge clutter, discount banners, noisy layouts)
- Hyperscaler console clones (AWS/Azure look) — the entire point is owning your own stack, not theirs

## Design Principles

1. **Sovereignty is the message — make ownership visible.** Lead with what the operator controls (their hardware, their brand, their customers). Every section should reinforce "this is yours," not "subscribe to ours."
2. **Authoritative restraint.** Weighty, precise, confident. Motion guides attention and never fights legibility. Taste over volume.
3. **Stay inside the house system.** `index.html` uses the legacy design system (tokens: `#080E1C` canvas, `#4A7830→#8BBF3C` accent, classes `.sec`, `.ey`, `.btn-p`, `.btn-g`, `.grad`). Build on what exists; don't invent parallel structure.
4. **Earn the technical reader's trust.** Concrete substance — layer breakdown, vendor comparisons, go-live steps — must be accurate and specific, not marketing vapor.
5. **No vendor-cliché tells.** Steer clear of the four anti-references above. Green accent is for large text, icons, and fills only; body text stays in fog grey for contrast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. `prefers-reduced-motion` must be respected on all animations. All interactive elements need keyboard and focus-indicator support.
